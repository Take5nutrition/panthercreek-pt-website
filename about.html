Practice History

For more than 35 years, PantherCreek Physical Therapy & Balance has served Clackamas County on one unwavering belief: that physical therapy done right — hands on, one on one, in a private room — can be genuinely life-changing.

PantherCreek Physical Therapy & Balance was founded in 1989 by Brant Lewis, PT, COMT, CFMT, on a belief that patients in Clackamas County deserved something different — care that is hands on, one on one, and delivered in the privacy of their own treatment room. Not a shared gym. Not a hand-off to an aide. Real therapy, from a real therapist, every single visit.

Brant earned his B.S. in Physical Therapy from Loma Linda University and has spent the decades since in continuous pursuit of clinical excellence — earning his Certified Functional Manual Therapy (CFMT) designation after four years of intensive coursework and a grueling five-day examination. His clinical focus spans sports injuries, post-surgical rehabilitation, neurologic conditions, and vestibular and balance disorders.

We've grown steadily over more than three decades, expanding to multiple locations across Clackamas County, but our approach has never changed. Every therapist we bring on must meet two standards: clinical excellence and a genuinely warm, caring personality. You can teach technique. You can't teach someone to care.

Our Mission

Why we show up
every day.

We help people move better, recover faster, and live stronger — through personalized, evidence-based care and exceptional patient experiences. Every decision we make flows from that promise.

Expertise that keeps up with science

Our therapists hold advanced clinical degrees and specialty certifications — and we invest constantly in continuing education. Physical therapy research moves fast. Our team moves with it.

Caring is a hiring requirement

Every therapist must pass two tests: clinical excellence and genuine human kindness. You deserve someone who cares about your life outside these walls, not just your pain level inside them.

Consistency that's part of the treatment

You see the same therapist every visit. No hand-offs. No re-explaining your history. That consistency isn't just convenient — it's clinically better.
