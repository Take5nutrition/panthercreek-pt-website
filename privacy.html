Privacy Policy

How PantherCreek Physical Therapy & Balance collects, uses, and protects your information.

Last updated: July 8, 2026  ·  Effective: July 8, 2026

Two separate privacy documents govern your information at PantherCreek PT. This page covers information collected through our website only. Your clinical health information — medical records, diagnoses, treatment notes, and billing — is governed by our HIPAA Notice of Privacy Practices, which you receive and sign at your first appointment. Please ask our front desk for a copy at any time.

1. Who We Are

PantherCreek Physical Therapy & Balance ("PantherCreek," "we," "our," or "us") is a HIPAA-covered entity operating three physical therapy clinics in Clackamas County, Oregon — in Clackamas, Molalla, and Estacada. We are subject to the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 (HIPAA) and its implementing regulations.

2. HIPAA and Your Protected Health Information

HIPAA grants you rights over your Protected Health Information (PHI) — any information that identifies you and relates to your health condition, treatment, or payment for treatment. Your PHI is collected and maintained in the clinic setting, not through this website. Our full HIPAA Notice of Privacy Practices explains:

  • How we use and disclose your PHI for treatment, payment, and healthcare operations.
  • Disclosures we are permitted or required to make without your authorization (e.g., public health reporting, law enforcement, court orders).
  • Your rights to access, amend, restrict, or receive an accounting of disclosures of your PHI.
  • How to file a complaint with us or with the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services (HHS) Office for Civil Rights if you believe your privacy rights have been violated.

To request a copy of our HIPAA Notice of Privacy Practices, call (503) 698-5500 or ask at the front desk at any location.

3. Information Collected Through This Website

This website does not collect clinical or medical information. The only personal information we collect online is what you voluntarily submit:

  • Appointment request forms — name, phone number, email address, preferred location, and preferred appointment time. Used solely to contact you and schedule your visit.
  • Contact form submissions — name, email address, phone number, and your message. Used only to respond to your inquiry.
  • Usage data — browser type, pages visited, and referring URL, collected automatically through standard server logs. Not linked to your identity.

Any health details you voluntarily include in a message or appointment request are used only to route your inquiry to the right care team. This information is not entered into your medical record until you become a patient and complete your clinical intake in person.

4. How We Use Website Information

  • To contact you regarding your appointment request or inquiry.
  • To provide information about our services.
  • To improve our website content and functionality.
  • To comply with applicable legal obligations.

We do not sell, rent, trade, or share your personal information with third parties for marketing or advertising purposes.

5. How We Share Website Information

We may share website-collected information only in the following limited circumstances:

  • Service providers — vendors who help us operate this website (web hosting, email delivery) under written agreements that restrict them from using your data for any other purpose.
  • Legal requirements — when required by law, subpoena, or court order, or to protect the rights, property, or safety of our patients, staff, or the public.
  • Business transfers — in the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of the practice, data would transfer to the successor entity under the same privacy commitments.

6. Business Associates

We work with certain third-party service providers ("Business Associates" under HIPAA) who may have access to PHI in the course of providing services to us — such as billing companies and electronic health record systems. All Business Associates are required to sign a Business Associate Agreement (BAA) and are legally bound to protect your PHI under HIPAA standards.

7. Your Rights Under HIPAA

As a patient, you have the following rights regarding your PHI held by our clinic. To exercise any of these rights, contact us in writing at any location or by phone:

  • Right to Access — request a copy of your medical record and billing information.
  • Right to Amend — request corrections to your PHI if you believe it is inaccurate or incomplete.
  • Right to Restrict — request limits on how we use or disclose your PHI.
  • Right to Confidential Communications — request that we contact you by a specific method or at a specific address.
  • Right to an Accounting of Disclosures — obtain a list of certain disclosures we have made of your PHI.
  • Right to Complain — file a complaint with us or with the HHS Office for Civil Rights at hhs.gov/ocr. We will not retaliate against you for filing a complaint.

8. Data Security

We implement administrative, technical, and physical safeguards consistent with HIPAA Security Rule requirements to protect information from unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. Our website uses HTTPS encryption for all data transmitted between your browser and our servers. No internet transmission is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

9. Cookies and Tracking

Our website may use cookies to maintain functionality and analyze traffic patterns. We do not use cookies to collect personal or health information. You may disable cookies in your browser settings; some website features may not function properly if you do.

10. Third-Party Links

Our website may link to third-party sites such as insurance portals or Google Maps. We are not responsible for the privacy practices of those sites and encourage you to review their privacy policies before submitting any personal information.

11. Children's Privacy

This website is not directed to children under age 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children online. If you believe a child under 13 has submitted personal information through this website, contact us immediately and we will promptly delete it.

12. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically. When we do, we will revise the effective date at the top of this page. Continued use of our website after changes are posted constitutes your acceptance of the revised policy.

13. Contact Us & How to File a Complaint

For questions about this Privacy Policy, to exercise your patient rights, or to file a privacy complaint with our practice:

  • PantherCreek Physical Therapy & Balance
  • Phone: (503) 698-5500
  • In person at any of our four Clackamas County locations

To file a complaint with the federal government: U.S. Department of Health & Human Services — Office for Civil Rights. You will not be penalized or retaliated against for filing a complaint.

This Privacy Policy addresses website data collection only. It is not a substitute for a formal HIPAA compliance review. Consult a qualified HIPAA attorney or compliance officer for clinical compliance questions.
