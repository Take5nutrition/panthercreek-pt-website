Parkinson's Disease PT

Move more.
Live better with Parkinson's.

Specialized physical therapy for Parkinson's disease — improving gait, balance, strength, and daily independence through amplitude-based exercise and expert neurological rehab.

What We Do

PT built around the specific challenges of Parkinson's.

Parkinson's takes away amplitude — the size and speed of movement. Our treatment fights back by training big, fast, and intense. We combine LSVT BIG protocols, gait and balance training, and our Rock Steady Boxing program to help you stay active and independent.

Amplitude-Based Movement Training

Parkinson's makes movements smaller and slower. We train the opposite — large, exaggerated movements that retrain the nervous system to produce normal-sized motion again.

Gait & Balance Rehabilitation

Cueing strategies, gait retraining, and progressive balance exercises that reduce freezing episodes, improve stride length, and significantly lower fall risk.

Rock Steady Boxing Program

A non-contact boxing exercise program specifically designed for people with Parkinson's — using forced-intensity exercise that research suggests may slow symptom progression.

Why It Matters

High-intensity exercise may slow the progression of Parkinson's disease.

Research from Cleveland Clinic and others confirms that intense, regular exercise drives neuroplastic changes in people with Parkinson's. The earlier you start and the more consistently you train, the better the long-term outcome.

Conditions Treated

Parkinson's symptoms we address.

Our team targets the motor symptoms that most affect daily life and independence for people living with Parkinson's disease.

Tremor

Resting and action tremors that interfere with fine motor tasks and daily activities.

Bradykinesia

Slowness of movement that affects everything from walking to facial expression.

Rigidity

Muscle stiffness and resistance that limits range of motion and causes discomfort.

Gait Abnormalities

Shuffling, reduced arm swing, and difficulty initiating steps.

Freezing of Gait

Sudden inability to continue walking — a major fall risk and independence limiter.

Postural Instability

Impaired righting reflexes that make falls more likely and recovery harder.

Our Process

A structured fight against Parkinson's progression.

1

Parkinson's-Specific Assessment

We evaluate your motor function, balance, gait, strength, and fall risk using validated Parkinson's assessment tools to establish your starting point.

2

Amplitude-Based Movement Retraining

Large, exaggerated exercises retrain the nervous system to produce bigger, faster, more normal movements — fighting the core pattern of Parkinson's progression.

3

Balance & Fall Prevention

Progressive balance training addresses the postural instability that makes falls so dangerous for people with Parkinson's.

4

Ongoing Maintenance & Progression

Parkinson's is progressive — so your program evolves with it. We build a long-term exercise plan that keeps you moving, adapting, and ahead of the disease.
